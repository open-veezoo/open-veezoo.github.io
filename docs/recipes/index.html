Recipes: How to Teach Veezoo

These recipes will help you take the most out of Veezoo. We suggest going one by one and applying those that may seem relevant for your data.

Natural Language Recipes#

synonym: I want to say 'NYC', instead of 'New York City'

onto.Person: I want to say 'who' and see 'customers'

onto.Location: I want to say 'where' and see 'countries'

creation_date: I want to ask about the age of something based on a date

default_date: If I say 2018, I mean Order Date in 2018

default_order: If I say 'top 10 orders', I mean 'top 10 orders by profit'

default_aggregation: When I say 'temperature per year', I want the average temperature per year, not the sum

Visualization Recipes#

display_with: I want to say X (e.g. customers) and see Y (e.g. nationality) as well

Knowledge Graph Design Recipes#

I want to define a custom boolean, e.g. 'orders.QUANTITY > 100' means 'big'

I want to import a varchar column as a date in Veezoo, e.g. TO_DATE(date_str, 'YYYYMMDD')

I want to transform a numeric column in Veezoo, e.g. 'orders.price_in_EUR * 1.1' means 'price_in_CHF'

I need to join two tables without foreign keys set

I want to hide a junction/intermediate table from my Veezoo users

I need to join two tables over a third table

I need to join two tables with a complicated join condition

I have everywhere the same column (e.g. Status), but I just want to see it once in Veezoo

I want to bucketize a numeric column into ranges, e.g. classroom.SIZE into '1 - 10', '10 - 100' and '100+'
